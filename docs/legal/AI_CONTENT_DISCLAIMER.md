# AI Content Disclaimer

**Effective date:** June 11, 2026

Babinom shows etymological meanings, origins, and translations for the names in its catalog. A meaningful portion of this content is produced or assisted by artificial intelligence ("AI"), specifically large-language models from OpenAI (currently gpt-4o-mini). This Disclaimer explains what that means and how to interpret what you see.

---

## What is AI-generated in the App

- **English meanings** for canonical names that were not in our curated dictionary at launch (roughly 14 500 of ~14 800 entries).
- **Translations** of every English meaning into the 10 non-English locales the App supports (Dutch, German, French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, Simplified Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Modern Standard Arabic).

In total, roughly **97% of the meanings shown in Babinom are produced or translated by an AI model.** The remaining ~3% are either editorially curated by us or sourced from public-domain references such as Wikidata.

Each meaning row in our database is tagged with its source (`curated-core`, `external:wikidata`, or `openai:<model-name>`) so we know which content came from where.

## How accurate is AI-generated content

AI language models produce plausible-sounding text. **They are not authoritative reference works.** Specifically:

- Meanings may be incomplete, oversimplified, or one-sided
- Origins may be assigned to the most common tradition for a name when multiple cultures use it
- Translations may use phrasings that are grammatically correct but culturally unidiomatic
- Religious, mythological, or historical references may be summarised in ways that experts in those traditions would refine or contest
- Rare or recently-coined names may produce confident-sounding but invented etymologies (we filter for self-rated low-confidence rows, but residual errors are possible)

We mark each meaning with the model's self-assessed confidence; meanings below a threshold are flagged in our database for human review.

## What you should NOT use it for

You should not rely on the meanings shown in Babinom for:

- Legal naming, birth certificate, or registry decisions
- Religious naming traditions, where specific scriptural or community guidance matters
- Genealogical research where source provenance is required
- Academic linguistic or historical work
- Tattoos, family heirlooms, or other permanent commitments where accuracy matters

For any of the above, please consult an authoritative reference (a published etymological dictionary, religious authority, or qualified naming specialist).

## How we try to be accurate

- We use a low-temperature setting (0.2) when generating meanings, which favours consistency over creativity
- We require the model to self-rate confidence and mark low-confidence entries for review
- We validate every output against basic format rules (length, no refusal phrases, no tautologies)
- We prefer human-curated and Wikidata-sourced meanings over AI-generated meanings when both exist for the same name, via a source-priority system
- We re-run the pipeline periodically as models improve

## How to report an error

If you find a meaning that's wrong, misleading, or culturally insensitive:

- Tap the name in the App to open the detail view
- Use the "Report" or "Send feedback" option (or email [support@babinom.com](mailto:support@babinom.com))
- Tell us the name, the meaning you saw, and what's wrong with it

We review reports and update the catalog when justified.

## Our use of AI providers

We currently use **OpenAI** as our language-model provider. Meanings and translations are generated server-side by us before reaching your device; we do not call OpenAI from your phone or share your personal data with them during generation. The names sent for enrichment are names already in our catalog, not data tied to your account.

For information on what data is collected and processed in connection with your use of Babinom, see our [Privacy Policy](https://www.babinom.com/privacy).
